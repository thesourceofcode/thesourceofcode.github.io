# The history of credit
Focusing on the "credit" part of Credit Card, let's focus on the origins of the concept of credit.

The concept of credit is nearly as old as civilization itself. It's not hard to see why the concept of credit arises
naturaly in agrarian societies. Farming needs several inputs, seeds, water, fertilizer, labour while the result of this input will take several months
to come to fruition. During this time the farmer can utilize credit to pay for the inputs and pay the bill after the harvest.

So its no surprise that there are ample references to credit across many ancient civilizations.

Example of credit transaction, doodhwalas in india provide milk daily and take payment at end of month

## Diner's Club
McNamara, Bloomingdale and Snyder started it. First transaction made on 8 feb 1950

Features of this card:
- Card Number
- Cardholder Name
- Signature
- Expiry Date

### A charge card transaction in 1950
After the bill was created. The person would give the card to the waiter.

The waiter would create a slip for the credit card transaction, noting
down the credit card number. He would then ask the patron to sign the slip

At this point the transaction was done from the customer's side.

The merchant would send the slips to Diner's club for payment

Diner's club would validate the slips and then issue the payment to the merchant
at the end of the month.

At the same time, the card holder would receive a bill from Diner's club
at the end of the month that he was supposed to pay in full since
this was actually a charge card.

Some primitive mechanisms to prevent frud were present. The waiter could
match the signature on the card to the one on the slip, he could
verify the name on the card by asking for some identification

Floor limits were set, above which cahiers would call diner's club
for authorization

Hot lists for blocked cards were regularly printed and circulated

Naturally due to so many manual steps involved the process was slow and error-prone



But diner's club was a charge card, the innovation of revolving credit was added by launch of bank cards in the BankAmericard

## Magnetic Stripe
The first major innovaion in credit card technology, the magnetic
stripe allowed card readers that could do near-real-time authorization
by reading the card details stored in the magnetic strip and sending
the data over telephone lines to authorize the transaction

## Automated Teller Machine (ATM)
The 1970s brought with it a wave of technological innovations, one of
which was the ATM. This machine could do many functions
of the bank. Credit cards could be used to withdraw and deposit money
and other stuff.
With the advent of ATMs came another invention, the humble Debit Card.
It looks like a credit card, feels like a credit card but it works
differently. Rather than being atached to a line of credit, debit cards
used the customer's bank account as its source of funds.
